# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Burned-out solo developers maintaining 10+ OSS repositories with no time to spare.
They are skeptical of hype, allergic to complexity, and have been burned by tools that require constant babysitting.
They arrive with one question: "Will this actually save me time, or create more work?"
Their context is GitHub notifications at 11pm, a backlog of stale issues,
and zero interest in reading a 40-page manual before they decide.

## Product Purpose

Night Brownie automates the boring parts of OSS repository maintenance — issue triage, labeling, responses —
so maintainers can focus on code.
It acts as a dedicated AI co-maintainer with its own GitHub identity, running always-on in the background.
Success looks like: a maintainer installs Night Brownie, configures one repo,
and has issues triaged without writing code in under 30 minutes.
The docs site exists to help visitors quickly determine if Night Brownie is the right tool for them,
then move them toward a walkthrough or deeper feature exploration.

## Brand Personality

Stealthy, efficient, trusted.
The "brownie" metaphor is literal: a helpful household spirit from folklore who tidies up while everyone sleeps.
Night Brownie doesn't need a spotlight; it needs a job to do.
It is the quiet competence of a clean hearth and a sorted inbox.
Not a loud "AI Assistant" — a quiet midnight helper.

## Anti-references

- Typical AI startup landing pages: gradient blobs, hero metrics, "10x your productivity" copy, glassmorphism cards,
    animated counters.
- Generic developer-tool dark modes that look like GitHub Docs with a dark theme swap.
- Overly friendly and approachable tones that feel like a SaaS onboarding flow.
- High-fantasy or whimsical "magic" tropes (no wands, no sparkles).
    The magic is in the efficiency, not the aesthetic.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn trust in the first paragraph.** The user is skeptical.
    Every claim must be concrete and specific — no vague promises about "streamlining workflows."
    Show what Night Brownie actually does.
2. **Midnight Clarity.**
    The interface should be optimized for late-night work.
    High legibility, deep contrast, and a palette that feels like a quiet room, not a bright office.
3. **Biolume Guidance.**
    Use accents (cyan and violet) sparingly to guide the eye toward the most important information or actions.
    Inspired by the mascot's glow, these are fixed points of orientation that cut through the night.
4. **Unobtrusive Utility.**
    The design should stay out of the way.
    Night Brownie is about getting the job done without being seen.
    Minimize UI friction and maximize information density where it counts.
5. **Reliable Magic.**
    The "magic" of AI should feel like a reliable utility, not a fickle trick.
    Design for predictability and transparency in what the agent is doing.
